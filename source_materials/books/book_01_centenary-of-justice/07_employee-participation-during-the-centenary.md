# Chapter 7: Employee Participation During the Centenary

*[Not only magistrates but also ordinary employees participated in the 100ᵗʰ birthday celebrations of the Supreme Court. The Court officials and staff were themselves deeply involved in various projects. In this chapter, Justice Panganiban pays tribute to them in a speech he delivered on February 14, 2001, in the Court of Appeals Auditorium.]*

**M***agandang hapon po sa inyong lahat. Binabati ko kayo ng isang Maligayang Araw ng mga Puso!  Happy Valentine!  Itinapat po nating talaga ang ating pagdiriwang para sa ating mga pinuno at mga manggagawa ng Kataastaasang Hukuman, Hukuman ng Apelasyon at Kagawaran ng Katarungan ngayong araw ng Valentine, upang idiin na ang mga pinakamalapit sa puso ng Supreme Court ay ang mga kawaning naglilingkod sa hustisya.  Alam na po nating lahat na ang ika-sandaang anibersaryo ng Korte Suprema ay sa ika-labing isa ng Hunyo ng kasalukuyang taon.  At ang ating pagdiriwang ngayon ay isang pagpapasinaya sa anibersaryong iyan.*

The centenary celebrations include all types of activities — intellectual, cultural, historical, educational and physical — participated in by all sectors of society including judges, lawyers, students, educators and all court employees in all regions in the Philippines.

Two weeks ago, on January 26 to 27, about a thousand officials and employees of Judicial Region 7 held their two-day centenary celebrations in Cebu City. A few days ago on February 9, the judges and judicial personnel of Dumaguete City, together with the academic community of Silliman University, celebrated the centenary with a grand debate between the Ateneo de Manila and the Silliman colleges of law. What specifically impressed me was the fact that more than 800 people each paid a P50 admission fee to be able to listen to this debate, which lasted from 8:00 p.m. to 10:30 p.m.

I mention these events, because I want to stress the Centenary Executive Committee’s special concern for the involvement of our employees in our centennial activities. Apart from this cultural presentation today, which is managed by our Cultural Committee headed by Justice Minerva P. Gonzaga-Reyes, our employees have held various other centenary programs.

On September 21, 2000, the Supreme Court Clinic Services and the SC Employees Association conducted a Basic Life Support Training Course; and on November 9, 2000, a Seminar-Workshop on Cancer Awareness. On November 17, we opened the Employees’ Sports Festival; while on December 7, the Medical Clinic and the SC Employees Association, in coordination with Pfizer Philippines, conducted a seminar on “Sexual Dysfunction Among Lay People.”

Future activities lined up by the Employees’ Participation Committee, headed by Attys. Luz Puno and Marissa Villarama, include a blood-letting program on February 23 plus seminars on “Stress Management” and “Dangers of Smoking.” As we approach the centenary day on June 11, loyalty awards will be given to all employees who have rendered at least ten years of service. Monetary awards and certificates will be given. And of course we have the Search for Special Centennial Awardees for Excellence, under a Committee chaired by Justices Josue N. Bellosillo and Jose Y. Feria, for judges and judicial personnel in all judicial regions.

A major highlight of our centenary celebrations is the implementation of a mass housing program for judicial employees. This is being attended to by a sub-committee headed by Justice Bernardo P. Pardo. Project sites have been inspected and negotiations are now being held with PAG-IBIG to enable employees to avail themselves of more favorable interest rates, similar to those given to policemen in their mass housing program.

I am mentioning all of these to show that, in our lineup of centenary projects, we have given high priority to those involving our work force. After all, without our employees, we cannot speak of the judiciary and the justice system. We all know that an office, however exalted or however lowly, can be effective only when it has the support and the cooperation of the people composing it. In the final analysis, therefore, the most important components of judicial dispensation and thus of our centennial celebrations are the men and women who compose the judicial branch of government; namely, all of us in this hall today.

It is in this spirit of belongingness, of oneness in celebrating the 100th year of our common desire to dispense quality justice, that I greet all of you. Let us, for this afternoon, set aside our more sanguine concerns. Let us all relax and enjoy the music and the dances especially prepared for all of us. *Dito po sa Supreme Court at Court of Appeals ay pwedeng ngumiti, pwedeng mag-**relax **at wala pong palalabasin! Kaya’t umupo po tayong maigi, makinig sa mga awitin ng mga koro ng **Supreme Court**, **Court of Appeals at Department of Justice. Magsama-sama po tayong magpugay at magbigay parangal sa lahat ng naninilbihan sa hustisya ng bayan.*

*Maraming salamat po.*
