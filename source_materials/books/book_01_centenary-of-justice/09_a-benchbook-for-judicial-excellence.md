# Chapter 9: A Benchbook for Judicial Excellence

*[One of the more visual projects completed during the centenary is the *Benchbook*, which contains in one single volume all the relevant information a judge needs to know while presiding during a trial. This book was formally launched on January 20, 2001, during a simple program held at the new SC Centenary Building. Here are Justice Panganiban’s short Closing Remarks during that occasion.]*

	108            A CENTENARY OF JUSTICE	A BENCHBOOK FOR JUDICIAL EXCELLENCE   109

**T**he Supreme Court, as many of us know, is celebrating its 100th anniversary on June 11, 2001. To commemorate this historic event, more than 50 projects and programs have been lined up from June 11, 2000 to June 11, 2001.1 They include all types of activities — intellectual, cultural, historical, educational and physical — participated in by all sectors of society especially judges and other judicial personnel, lawyers, educators, students and the public at large in all regions of the country.

Our goals are, *first*, to communicate to everyone the role of the judiciary in nation-building during the last 100 years; and, *second*, to focus on and encourage participation in the ongoing judicial reform program of the Supreme Court. Our overall theme is “Katarungan at Bayan, Magpakailanman.” By this we emphasize our allegiance to one cause, justice; and to one entity, the nation.

Essentially, the Judicial Reform Program aims to uplift the judges themselves, their judicial tools and their working environment. The reforms are designed to enhance what I call the four “Ins” of a great magistrate; namely, integrity, intelligence, independence and industry.

Indeed, we need ethical and competent magistrates who will rise to the high standards demanded by our people. To achieve this, we need a program of continuing legal education; and, in particular, a handy reference book that can be instantly used by judges to resolve, on the spot, issues and questions that may be raised in the course of a trial. Verily, the 

*Benchbook*, which Chief Justice Hilario G. Davide Jr. calls the judicial bible, is a necessary component of our Judicial Reform Program and a major part of our centenary activities.

This book is a much-needed tool of the men and women in black robes, because it assures them of up-to-date, reliable and dependable information to back up their decisions and actions. It gives them strength and confidence in making their rulings and, generally, in dealing with their work.

Our Constitution requires judges to render written decisions in which the facts and the applicable law are correctly, clearly, completely, and concisely explained. This *Benchbook* — whether in print, CD ROM, disc or webpage version — is an indispensable guide for the speedy dispensation of quality justice. Indeed, it is a necessary tool to enhance judicial excellence.
